MAMMOOTTY : THE GREAT ACTOR



Mammootty, born Muhammad Kutty Panaparambil Ismail on September 7, 1951, in Chandiroor, Kerala, is a legendary Indian actor and film producer predominantly associated with Malayalam cinema. With a career spanning over five decades, he has acted in more than 400 films across various languages, including Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Hindi, and English.

Career Highlights Mammootty's acting debut was in 1971 with an uncredited role in Anubhavangal Paalichakal. His first credited role came in 1973's Kaalachakram. He gained significant recognition in the early 1980s, with notable performances in films like Ahimsa (1981), which earned him his first Kerala State Film Award. Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, he delivered a series of critically acclaimed and commercially successful films, including Oru Vadakkan Veeragatha (1989), Mathilukal (1990), Vidheyan (1994), and Ponthan Mada (1994).

In 2000, Mammootty portrayed Dr. B. R. Ambedkar in the bilingual biopic Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar, earning him his third National Film Award for Best Actor.

Mammootty has won the National Film Award for Best Actor three times:

  1. 1989 : for Oru Vadakkan Veeragatha and Mathilukal
  2. 1993 : for Vidheyan and Ponthan Mada
  3. 1997 : for Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar (biopic in Hindi and English)
